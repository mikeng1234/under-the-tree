Fewer laps today

The park is quieter than past weeks. Fewer shoes on the path, fewer shadows crossing the oval. The crisis in the Middle East, the gas prices climbing — maybe that's why. The world tightens its belt and the park feels it too, in absences.

What the joggers carry

What do they think about, the ones still circling the oval? Are they outrunning something — a deadline, a memory, a version of themselves they don't want to be? Or is it simpler than that. Maybe they think of dinner. Maybe they think of nothing at all. I envy that — the mercy of a quiet mind at full stride.

Golden hour

The sun hangs thirty degrees above the horizon, golden and unhurried in its leaving. Everything it touches turns warm — the grass, the path, the joggers' shoulders. Soon it will dip. But right now the light is the kindest thing in the park.
